Add friends to your Friends List in Minecraft: Java Edition to see when they’re online in Minecraft. Learn more about upcoming Friends List improvements at [Minecraft News, Articles, and Gaming Announcements](https://www.minecraft.net/en-us/article).

Here’s how the Friends List works:

- If you add a friend in Minecraft: Java Edition, it will also add them to your Xbox friends list. Existing Xbox friends will also show up as friends in Minecraft: Java Edition if they own the game.
- If you remove a friend in Minecraft: Java Edition, it will also remove them from your Xbox friends list.
- Your Xbox privacy & online safety settings affect your Minecraft settings. Change them at [Xbox Official Site: Consoles, Games and Community](https://www.xbox.com/en-US/user/settings/privacy-and-safety).
  - If your Xbox chat setting under Privacy \> You can communicate outside of Xbox with voice & text is set to **friends**, you will only see chat from players on your Friends List. Add a player as a friend or change it to **everyone** to see chat from all players in multiplayer games.

Change who can send you friend requests and your visibility from the **Options** \> **Online** \> **Friends List** menu in Minecraft.

- **Friends List**: This must be **ON** to use the Friends List. If you initially chose **Turn OFF** when you opened the Friends List, toggle it on here. If this setting is off, you will not be able to send or receive friend requests.
- **Allow Requests**: Turn this **OFF** to stop players from sending your friend requests.
- **In-Game Notification**: Set this **ON** to receive toast notifications and **OFF** to silence them.
- **Visibility**: **Full** will show when you’re online and what you’re playing; **hidden** will always show you as offline; and **limited** will display you as online to your friends, but not what you’re playing.
- **Xbox Settings**: Select this to modify your [Xbox Official Site: Consoles, Games and Community](https://www.xbox.com/en-US/user/settings/privacy-and-safety) privacy settings.

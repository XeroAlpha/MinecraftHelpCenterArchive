The audio controls in Minecraft: Java Edition have been designed to provide various options for customizing your in-game audio experience. To access these settings:

1.  Open Minecraft: Java Edition.
2.  Select **Options**.
3.  Select **Music & Sounds**.

From this menu, adjust settings for a variety of different audio channels:

## Volume Sliders

- **Master Volume**: Controls the overall volume of the entire game.
- **Music**: Changes the volume of Minecraft’s background soundtrack.
- **Jukebox/Note Blocks**: Controls the volume of jukebox music disks and note block sounds.
- **Weather**: Changes the volume for weather events such as rain and thunder.
- **Block**: Controls the volume for block interactions, such as breaking and placing blocks.
- **Hostile Mobs**: Manages the volume for all hostile mobs and bosses.
- **Friendly Mobs**: Changes the volume for all non-hostile mobs.
- **Players**: Controls the volume for sounds from player actions.
- **Ambient/Environment**: Changes the volume of ambient sounds occurring in the game environment.
- **Narrator/Voice**: Manages the volume of Minecraft’s text-to-speech narrator.  
- **UI**: Controls the volume for user interface sounds, such as menu and inventory navigation sounds.

## Other Sound Features

- **Device**: Lets you choose which audio output device Minecraft uses.
- **Closed Captions**: Displays text subtitles with directional indicators for nearby sounds while playing the game.
- **Directional Audio**: Enables stereo positional audio, making sounds appear from directions based on player position, so it’s easier to tell where whatever is making a sound is located.
- **Music Frequency** controls how often music plays in the background:  
  - **Default**: Music in the game world has a gap between music tracks.
  - **Frequent**: A shorter gap between music tracks in-game.
  - **Constant**: Music is constantly playing in the background.
- **Music Toast** toggles pop-up notifications when music starts with information about the track being played.
  - **Never**: No music toast is shown.
  - **Pause Menu**: A music toast is displayed in the in-game pause menu while a song is playing.
  - **Pause Menu and Toast**: The music toast is constantly displayed in the pause menu when a track is playing and appears in-game when it begins playing.

If you’re having problems joining or accepting a party invite/world, it may be related to conflicts with your device, the friend system, your account settings, or your network. Here are simple steps that solve most issues.

- **Add your friends**: If you don’t see the option to add players to your party, make sure that you [Add Friends to Your Friend List in Minecraft: Bedrock Edition](./Add-Friends-to-Your-Friend-List-in-Minecraft-Bedrock-Edition.md) first.
- **Ask for/send another invite**: If the party leader or a party member isn’t fully loaded into Minecraft when a party invite is sent, the invite may not be available for the party member. Party members who quit the game will also need an invite to rejoin a party. Have the party leader send another invite to solve both of these scenarios.
- **Update to the latest version of Minecraft: Bedrock Edition**: Parties only work on Minecraft: Bedrock Edition. All players must also be on the same version (release or preview) and build. See our [Download & Install articles](https://help.minecraft.net/hc/en-us/sections/27166490706957) for platform-specific walkthroughs to manually update your game.
- **Use a supported platform**: Parties are supported on all Minecraft: Bedrock Edition platforms, except Android and iOS (coming soon). Switch to a non-mobile platform to create or join a party.
- **Check the party leader’s world settings**: If the party leader’s world is set to friends-only, party members who are not their friends will not be able to join and receive the Kelp error. Change player access to **friends of friends** or send the other party members direct invites so they can join.
- **Check your party settings**: If you are not the party leader and trying to send an invite, you may be blocked from doing so if the party leader doesn’t allow party members to send invites. If you’re unable to receive invites, you may need to change your receive party invite settings from **friends only** to **everyone**. If the party leader changed any settings, they need to disband and recreate the party to enable them.
- **Check your account settings**: If you have a child account, you may need your guardian to change your account settings to join or participate in a party. See [Troubleshooting Errors Playing Minecraft Multiplayer Games with a Child Account](../Account-Settings/Troubleshooting-Errors-Playing-Minecraft-Multiplayer-Games-with-a-Child-Account.md) and [Managing Multiplayer Game Access for a Child Account in Minecraft](../Account-Settings/Managing-Multiplayer-Game-Access-for-a-Child-Account-in-Minecraft.md).
- **Restart and wait up to 5 minutes**: If you made a change to your party settings, account settings, or friend status, it may take a restart and/or time for that change to be communicated between your Microsoft and Minecraft accounts. Restart Minecraft and wait a few minutes before further troubleshooting.
- **Troubleshoot your network connection**: Verify your network meets all requirements outlined in [Troubleshoot Minecraft Network Connection Errors](../Error-Code-Troubleshooting/Troubleshoot-Minecraft-Network-Connection-Errors.md). If you receive an error code, see [Named Error Code Troubleshooting for Minecraft: Bedrock Edition](../Error-Code-Troubleshooting/Named-Error-Code-Troubleshooting-for-Minecraft-Bedrock-Edition.md).

Whether you’re the party leader or a party member, you may want to manage your party members or leave a party. If you’re done playing Minecraft for the day, the easiest way to leave a party is to quit the game: exiting Minecraft will automatically remove you from any party you’re with, even if you’re the party leader.

If you still want to play Minecraft—but solo or only with certain players—here’s how to leave or manage your party.

## Manage a Minecraft Party

Manage players in your Minecraft party from the social menu.

1.  Select the **Social** button in the top-right.
2.  Select the **Party** tab (group silhouette).
3.  Select the kebab menu next to the player’s name and choose from the following:
    - **Promote to leader**: This will promote the party member to leader.
      - The party will be prompted to travel to the new party leader if they're in a different world.
      - Party leader settings are remembered from when the party was created, so the new party leader needs to reform the party if they want different settings.
    - **Remove from party**: This will remove the party member from the party. You will also be prompted with the option to block the player. The former party member will be notified that they were removed.

You can also access the standard friend/player controls from the same menu and favorite, mute, block, report, or remove friend.

## Leave a Minecraft party

1.  Select the **Social** button in the top-right.
2.  Select the **Party** tab (group silhouette).
3.  Select the red **leave party** button.

A Minecraft biome is a region with its own unique geography, plants, mobs, and other characteristics. It typically reflects a real-world biome, such as a desert or forest. While Minecraft: Bedrock Edition and Minecraft: Java Edition share most of the same biomes, some are Java Edition-exclusive. Here are all the biomes you can encounter in Minecraft!

## Overworld biomes

### Plains

- **Plains**: A relatively flat area that can have trees. You may find villages and farm animals, like horses.
- **Snowy plains**: A snow and ice-covered, mostly flat area with few trees and hills. Home to rabbits and polar bears. You may find Igloos.
- **Ice spikes**: A variant of the snowy plains that have large icy spikes and glaciers.
- **Sunflower plains**: A variant of the plains biome and the only one where sunflowers spawn.
- **Mushroom fields**: An island made of mycelium where big mushrooms grow. It is the only home of mooshrooms.
- **Savanna**: A flat, dry area with acacia and oak trees. Its tall grasses house llamas, armadillos, and warm animal variants.

### Woodlands

- **Forest**: The most common biome in Minecraft, filled with oak trees, birch trees, and various flowers. Wolves frequently appear.
- **Birch forest**: A variant of the forest biome filled only with birch trees. Wolves do not spawn here.
- **Old growth birch forest**: A variant of the birch forest with some much taller trees. You may discover trail ruins.
- **Dark forest**: A forest variant of dark oak trees that cover the sky. Hostile mobs may spawn during the day. Woodland mansions can appear.
- **Pale garden**: A rare variant of the dark forest and the only biome with pale oaks and pale moss. Creakings may appear.
- **Flower forest**: A variant of the forest biome with fewer trees, but many more flowers. You’ll see plenty of bees and rabbits.
- **Taiga**: A pine and spruce forest biome with ferns and berry bushes. Cold animal variants spawn here. Foxes and wolves are common. You may even find trail ruins.
- **Old growth spruce taiga**: A taiga biome variant with giant spruce trees covered in leaf blocks. The ground is made of podzol and course dirt and mushrooms are common. Lush caves are likely to generate underground.
- **Old growth pine taiga**: A taiga variant with taller, wider spruce trees. Mossy cobblestone, course dirt, and podzol are all around. Mushrooms are also common.
- **Snowy taiga**: This taiga variant is covered in snow. You may find igloos or trail ruins along with cold variant animals. Lush or dripstone caves may generate underground.
- **Jungle**: A biome dense with tall jungle and oak trees. Vines commonly cover blocks. You may find ocelots, parrots, pandas, and warm animal variants. Cocoa and melons—and jungle pyramids—are only found here.
- **Bamboo jungle**: A jungle variant covered in large jungle trees and thickets of bamboo. Pandas spawn in this area more often. Lush or dripstone caves are frequently found below.
- **Sparse jungle**: A much less dense variation of the jungle. All the same things appear, but there is more space between everything.
- **Grove**: A spruce tree biome that appears on mountains near forest biomes. Snow and powder snow cover the ground, with deep dark, dripstone, or lush caves frequently further below.
- **Cherry grove**: This small biome often generates in mountain foothills and features the distinctive cherry tree. Collect pink petals and cherry blocks.

### Caves

- **Deep dark**: A deep underground area covered in sculk blocks and the only place you will find ancient cities. Be careful of noise around sculk sensor and shrieker blocks, as they can summon the warden!
- **Dripstone caves**: These typically inland caves are the exclusive place to find dripstone blocks and pointed dripstone. Copper ore is abundant.
- **Lush caves**: Typically found below azalea trees. Explore caverns filled with moss, azalea bushes, cave vines, glow berries, dripleaf plants, and spore blossoms. Axolotls and tropical fish can appear here.
- **Sulfur caves**: An underground biome of sulfur and cinnabar blocks, shallow water pools, and sulfur cube spawns found under sulfur springs. Brave sulfur pools to collect the potent sulfur at the bottom.

### Mountains

- **Jagged peaks**: Found at the top of the tallest mountains, often extending beyond the reach of clouds. Underneath the snow, you can find stone, coal, iron, or emerald.
- **Frozen peaks**: A not-so-jagged area filled with ice, snow, and packed ice.
- **Stony peaks**: A peak variation that generates in warmer areas. No snow or goats appear here.
- **Snowy slopes**: Mountains in this biome are covered in snow and snow powder. Igloos can generate. Goats and rabbits spawn here.
- **Windswept hills**: A biome filled with steep cliffs, valleys, and floating islands. Llamas and emerald can appear.
- **Windswept forest**: A windswept hill variant that generates next to a forest. Instead of stone, it has an all-grass floor with trees.
- **Windswept gravelly hills**: A windswept hills variant mostly filled with gravel blocks.
- **Meadow**: A grassy biome with flowers that appear near mountains. If you find a tree here, it will always have a bee nest.
- **Stony shore**: This biome appears when a mountain generates directly next to an ocean. Lots of stone and potential for buried treasure.
- **Savanna Plateau**: A savanna that generates at a higher altitude in a mountainous area.
- **Windswept savanna**: A savanna in a mountainous area that has very steep cliffs that can reach high into the sky.

### Swamps

- **Swamp**: A dark hued area with shallow waters scattered around. Trees covered in vines can sometimes grow out of the water. Temperate frogs, blue orchids, and swamp huts appear here.
- **Mangrove swamp**: A swamp variant covered in more vegetation than normal swamps. Mangrove trees appear here, and mud blocks take up much of the floor. Warm frogs spawn here.

### Sandy areas

- **Badlands**: A desert made of red sand. Terracotta and stained terracotta can appear frequently.
- **Wooded badlands**: Coarse dirt and oak trees appear in this badlands variant.
- **Eroded badlands**: Badlands variant that has large spires of terracotta that shoot into the sky.
- **Beach**: Sandy areas where water meets land. Turtles spawn here and buried treasure can appear underground.
- **Snowy beach**: A beach variant that appears next to a frozen ocean. The sand is covered in snow and turtles do not appear here.
- **Desert**: A large biome with sand, sandstone, cacti, and the occasional fossil. Husks can spawn at nighttime. Desert villages, wells, and pyramids appear here.

### Water areas

- **River**: Filled with stretches of water blocks that cut through land and often lead into oceans. Salmon, squid, and drowned spawn in these waters.
- **Frozen river**: A river variant covered in ice that generates when a river cuts through snow biomes. Salmon and drowned still spawn underwater, and rabbits and polar bears appear on top of the ice.
- **Ocean**: A basic ocean biome of seagrass, kelp, and fish. There are warm, lukewarm, cold, and frozen variants.
- **Deep ocean:** Deeper than regular oceans. You can find underwater ravines with magma blocks that try to pull you down. There are lukewarm, cold, and frozen ocean variants.
- **Warm ocean variant**: These biome variants can naturally generate coral reefs. Find dolphins, pufferfish, and tropical fish.
- **Lukewarm ocean variants**: These biome variants do not contain coral reefs, but feature more seagrass, cod, salmon, and kelp.
- **Cold ocean variants**: These biome variants are a deep-blue hue and home less aquatic wildlife than other ocean biomes. Large groups of kelp can grow.
- **Frozen ocean variants**: These biome variants contain only salmon, squid, and ocean monuments. You can sometimes find polar bears.

## The Nether biomes

- **Nether wastes**: Netherrack is everywhere, with clumps of glowstone mixed in. Large lakes of lava are common, and you will see hostile ghast, piglin, and magma cube mobs.
- **Soul sand valley**: Soul sand and soul soil are in abundance here and will slow you down unless you have boots with the Soul Speed enchantment.
- **Basalt deltas**: Basalt and blackstone are plentiful and this is the only biome in The Nether where bastion remnants do not appear.
- **Crimson forest:** Warped and crimson fungus cover the landscape. The fungus structures have weeping vines and shroomlights. Bring gold to barter with the many piglins and hoglins.
- **Warped forest**: Only striders and endermen appear in these blue fungus areas. Warped nylium, warped roots, nether sprouts, and warped wart blocks can be commonly found.

## The End biomes

- **The End**: A central island of The End where the ender dragon is. End crystals sit at the top of large obsidian spires and endermen walk around the ground freely.
- **End barrens**: An outer portion of The End on the edge of its islands. Only endermen appear here.
- **End highlands**: An outer portion of The End where both end cities and chorus fruit appear. These are located at higher elevation than other biomes.
- **End midlands**: An outer portion of The End where end cities can appear, but chorus fruit does not.
- **Small end islands**: These small floating islands are barren but appear packed together.

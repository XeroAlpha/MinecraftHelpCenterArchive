The Drowned error means there was a problem signing in to your Microsoft account. Here are some simple steps to help resolve it.

## Refresh your account session

Follow the steps to [Reset Your Microsoft Account Sign-in in Minecraft](../Account-Sign-in/Reset-Your-Microsoft-Account-Sign-in-in-Minecraft.md). This will refresh your account session and is a potentially easy fix.

## Update to the latest Minecraft version

Another step that may solve the issue is to update to the latest version of Minecraft. Follow instructions for your platform to manually update:

- [Manually Update Minecraft on Mobile Devices](../Download-Install/Manually-Update-Minecraft-on-Mobile-Devices.md)
- [Manually Update Minecraft on Consoles](../Download-Install/Manually-Update-Minecraft-on-Consoles.md)
- [Manually Update Minecraft: Bedrock Edition on PC](../Download-Install/Manually-Update-Minecraft-Bedrock-Edition-on-PC.md)

## Check platform status

If your device’s platform has service issues, you may be unable to sign in. Check these service status sites to see if there are currently issues. If the platform is having service issues, you may need to wait until the service is back online before trying again.

- [Xbox](https://support.xbox.com/en-US/xbox-live-status)
- [PlayStation](https://status.playstation.com/en-us/)
- [Nintendo](https://www.nintendo.co.jp/netinfo/en_US/index.html)
- [Apple](https://www.apple.com/support/systemstatus/)
- [Google Play](https://status.play.google.com/)

## Change your Microsoft account credentials

If none of the other steps have resolved the issue for you, follow these steps to [Change your Microsoft account password](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/account-billing/change-your-microsoft-account-password-fdde885b-86da-2965-69fd-4871309ef1f1). Changing your Microsoft account password forces a fresh sign-in and should solve it.
